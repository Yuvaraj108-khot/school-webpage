Our Journey

History & Introduction

In the year 1953, Srimad Bhuvanendra High School (6th to 10th std. in Kannada medium) was started at the present campus with noble objectives of providing general education to all, irrespective of caste, creed, and religion, along with Sanskrit as an optional subject. Since then, our school has consistently secured excellent results in the S.S.L.C. Examination. Currently, the Kannada medium high school section serves about 110 students with 7 experienced teaching staff. In May 2023, our 50-year-old building was renovated at a cost of approximately Rs. 12 Lakh with the generous support of donors, primarily Can Fin Homes Ltd.


English medium sections from 8th to 10th std. were introduced between June 1998 to 2000 on a permanent, non-grant basis. This section currently has 127 students, supported by 6 teaching and 4 non-teaching staff members.


In 2004, to provide a complete educational path from pre-primary to high school on a single campus, classes for Nursery, LKG, UKG, and 1st to 7th std. were started. A separate building with modern facilities was constructed at a cost of Rs. 40 Lakhs through donor support. Our English Medium Higher Primary School now hosts 475 students with 15 teaching and 5 non-teaching staff members.


We take pride in our cultural heritage, with nearly 2/3 of our pupils learning Sanskrit. Additionally, since 2002, our in-house kitchen has been providing free, hygienic, and nutritious meals to all students under the mid-day meal program, with an annual expenditure of approximately Rs. 9 Lakhs.

Vision of Founder

Divinity of the saint and sublimity of nature have rendered the campus a balanced blending of an ideal surrounding meant for the all-round development of the young in their formative years. Located just 1 KM from the town, the campus has the advantage of fresh breeze and an unpolluted life necessary for a healthy body and mind.

Padmashree Dr. T. M. A. Pai

Dr. T. M. A. Pai (1898 – 1978), the wizard of Manipal, is the visionary whose farsightedness transformed a barren tiny village into a productive megatown. His message of hope to the poor and deprived resonates in our institution's values.
